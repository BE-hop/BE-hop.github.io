## Role Trend Summary

The strongest shared signal is that AI-facing design and product roles increasingly reward people who can translate domain judgment into structured systems: evaluation standards, data pipelines, product requirements, prototypes, and repeatable workflows.

Observed directions:

- Design-domain data product manager: build model evaluation systems for visual, UI, and brand-design tasks; define design-quality dimensions such as aesthetics, logic, expression, usability, and creativity; convert design expertise into measurable data and model-optimization strategies.
- AI product manager: analyze requirements, design features, produce prototypes, coordinate R&D and testing, track user feedback and data, and move products from 0 to 1 or from 1 to N.
- Kimi product intern: work on C-end AI products, multi-end experience, user research, data analysis, prompt usage, and conversion of creative ideas into usable product features.
- AI Agent product manager: research AI Agent and intelligent-agent cases, extract lightweight product ideas, coordinate with engineering, support global adaptation through overseas research, and form reusable methods and templates.
- AIGC creative product manager: apply AIGC to content and e-commerce scenarios, analyze search/browsing/conversion behavior, collaborate with algorithm and operations teams, and use multimodal tools for image, video, audio, and interactive creative generation.

## Website Strategy Implications

The English-facing site should position the owner as a design practitioner who can connect AI tools, product thinking, and visual/domain judgment. The site should not only show finished visuals; it should show how decisions are made, evaluated, prototyped, and iterated.

Priority content moves:

- Make RU-LineArt a flagship AI-assisted design workflow project. Emphasize problem definition, user scenario, technical workflow, output quality, iteration history, and design-tool value.
- Strengthen Works case studies with process evidence: brief, role, constraints, tools, decision logic, deliverables, outcome, and reflection.
- Add English-first blog posts around AI design tools, AIGC workflows, design evaluation, AI Agent/product thinking, and prototype-to-tool experiments.
- Add portfolio language that surfaces product judgment: user needs, pain points, prototype validation, technical collaboration, data-informed iteration, and reusable methods.
- Avoid hard-selling service copy. The strongest career signal is credible evidence, not aggressive conversion language.
